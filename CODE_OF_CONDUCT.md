# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Our standards for behavior are designed to create a safe and welcoming environment for all participants.

### Expected Behavior

- **Be Respectful**: Treat others with dignity and respect
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome newcomers and encourage diverse perspectives
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together to solve problems and build solutions
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and support learning
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional communication and conduct
- **Be Empathetic**: Consider the impact of your words and actions on others

### Prohibited Behavior

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, including but not limited to:
  - Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, physical appearance, body size, race, religion, or political affiliation
  - Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
  - Intimidation, stalking, or following
  - Sustained disruption of talks, events, or discussions

- **Discriminatory Language**: Use of slurs, derogatory terms, or exclusionary language

- **Personal Attacks**: Insults, ad hominem attacks, or personal criticism

- **Spam and Self-Promotion**: Excessive self-promotion, spam, or off-topic content

- **Confidentiality**: Sharing private or confidential information without consent

- **Disruptive Behavior**: Any behavior that disrupts the community's productive work

## Health Data Sensitivity

Given that Medly deals with health information, we have additional expectations:

### Health Data Guidelines

- **Privacy First**: Never ask users to share private health information in public channels
- **Respect Boundaries**: Be sensitive when discussing health-related topics
- **Professional Conduct**: Maintain professional standards when discussing medical topics
- **No Medical Advice**: Do not provide medical advice; direct users to healthcare professionals
- **Data Protection**: Never request or handle personal health data outside of secure channels

### Medical Discussion Guidelines

- **Disclaimer**: Always include disclaimers that discussions are not medical advice
- **Professional Sources**: Encourage consultation with qualified healthcare providers
- **Respect Privacy**: Never share personal health experiences without explicit consent
- **Supportive Environment**: Create a supportive space for health-related discussions

## Reporting

### Reporting Process

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report them through the following channels:

#### Primary Reporting
- **Email**: conduct@medly.app
- **GitHub Issues**: [Report an Issue](https://github.com/Iceman-Dann/Medly/issues)
- **Private Message**: Contact any maintainer directly

#### Emergency Reporting
For immediate threats or serious violations:
- **Email**: emergency@medly.app
- **Direct Message**: Any core team member

### What to Include in Your Report

- **Description**: What happened and when
- **Participants**: Who was involved
- **Context**: Any relevant context or screenshots
- **Impact**: How the behavior affected you or others
- **Preferred Resolution**: What you would like to see happen

### Confidentiality

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. We will:

- **Protect Privacy**: Keep your identity confidential if requested
- **Thorough Investigation**: Conduct a thorough and fair investigation
- **Timely Response**: Respond within 48 hours for most reports
- **Follow-up**: Keep you informed of the resolution process

## Enforcement

### Enforcement Process

Our enforcement process is designed to be fair, transparent, and effective:

#### Step 1: Initial Review
- **Immediate Assessment**: Evaluate the report within 24 hours
- **Severity Determination**: Classify the violation severity
- **Action Plan**: Determine appropriate response

#### Step 2: Investigation
- **Evidence Collection**: Gather relevant information and evidence
- **Witness Statements**: Interview involved parties when appropriate
- **Context Analysis**: Consider all relevant context and circumstances

#### Step 3: Resolution
- **Decision**: Make a fair and informed decision
- **Action**: Implement appropriate consequences
- **Communication**: Communicate resolution to involved parties

### Consequences

Consequences may include:

#### For Minor Violations
- **Warning**: Private warning and education
- **Monitoring**: Increased monitoring of behavior
- **Requirements**: Specific requirements for future participation

#### For Moderate Violations
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Probation**: Probationary period with specific conditions
- **Required Training**: Mandatory education on community standards

#### For Severe Violations
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from all community spaces
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement when appropriate
- **Public Statement**: Public announcement when necessary

### Appeals Process

If you believe an enforcement decision was made in error:

1. **Submit Appeal**: Email appeals@medly.app within 7 days
2. **Review Panel**: Your appeal will be reviewed by a different panel
3. **Decision**: Final decision within 14 days of appeal submission

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Standards

#### In Discussions
- **Stay On Topic**: Keep discussions relevant to the channel or thread
- **Use Clear Language**: Write clearly and concisely
- **Provide Context**: Give enough context for others to understand
- **Be Patient**: Allow time for responses, especially across time zones

#### In Code Reviews
- **Be Constructive**: Focus on improving the code, not criticizing the author
- **Explain Reasoning**: Provide clear explanations for suggested changes
- **Acknowledge Good Work**: Recognize well-written code and good practices
- **Be Open to Feedback**: Accept feedback on your own code gracefully

#### In Collaborative Work
- **Communicate Early**: Share progress and challenges early
- **Ask for Help**: Don't hesitate to ask for assistance
- **Share Knowledge**: Help others learn and grow
- **Celebrate Success**: Acknowledge team achievements and milestones

### Diversity and Inclusion

#### Commitment to Diversity
- **Welcome All**: Actively welcome participants from all backgrounds
- **Accommodate Needs**: Make reasonable accommodations for accessibility needs
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Be aware of and respect cultural differences
- **Language Inclusivity**: Use inclusive language and avoid assumptions

#### Inclusive Practices
- **Pronoun Respect**: Use correct pronouns and respect gender identity
- **Accessibility**: Ensure digital content is accessible
- **Time Zone Awareness**: Be considerate of global participants
- **Language Barriers**: Be patient with non-native English speakers

## Leadership Responsibilities

### Maintainer Expectations

Our community leaders are expected to:

#### Lead by Example
- **Model Behavior**: Demonstrate the behavior expected from others
- **Uphold Standards**: Enforce community standards consistently
- **Be Approachable**: Be available and approachable for community members
- **Stay Informed**: Keep current with best practices and community needs

#### Enforcement Duties
- **Respond Promptly**: Address reports and issues quickly
- **Be Fair**: Apply standards consistently and without bias
- **Document Actions**: Keep records of enforcement actions
- **Communicate Clearly**: Provide clear explanations for decisions

#### Community Building
- **Foster Growth**: Help community members learn and develop
- **Create Opportunities**: Provide opportunities for contribution and leadership
- **Celebrate Success**: Recognize and celebrate community achievements
- **Listen Actively**: Seek and consider community feedback

### Conflict Resolution

#### Internal Conflicts
- **Mediation**: Provide mediation for disputes between community members
- **Escalation**: Have clear escalation paths for serious conflicts
- **Documentation**: Document resolution processes and outcomes
- **Learning**: Use conflicts as learning opportunities for the community
